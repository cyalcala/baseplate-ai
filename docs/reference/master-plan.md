# Baseplate Master Plan

Date updated: 2026-05-17

## North Star

Baseplate becomes a trusted AI architecture decision layer.

The long-term product is not just a research archive. It is a system that helps builders answer:

- what should I build by default?
- when is the default wrong?
- what evidence supports this?
- what changed recently?
- what should I avoid overbuilding?

## First Wedge

Production RAG.

This wedge is narrow enough to validate and broad enough to matter. RAG builders repeatedly face decisions about retrieval, chunking, reranking, citations, evals, access control, GraphRAG, agentic RAG, cost, and latency.

## First Audience

Solo AI builders and very small teams building:

- document Q&A
- internal knowledge assistants
- customer support RAG
- research assistants
- product documentation assistants
- policy/legal/document intelligence tools

## First Product Shape

Start with decision artifacts, not a platform.

Initial public artifacts:

- RAG-001 decision page
- Architecture Diff #001
- retrieval architecture comparison
- evidence notes and source queue

Initial paid offer:

- USD 99 Baseplate RAG Architecture Review
- manual delivery
- 48 to 72 hour turnaround
- source-backed recommendation with caveats, eval plan, and "do not build yet" list

## Why This Path

It tests the hardest assumption first: whether builders value the decision layer.

It avoids spending weeks building software before knowing whether the judgment, framing, and deliverable are useful.

It creates assets that compound:

- public trust
- evidence bank
- repeatable decision templates
- paid review examples
- future playbook content
- future automation requirements

## 30-Day Plan

### Phase 1: Public Proof

Goal: prove one artifact is useful to real builders.

Tasks:

- final-polish RAG-001
- final-polish Architecture Diff #001
- share one artifact publicly or directly with builders
- ask whether it saves time or prevents a bad architecture choice
- record confusion, objections, and useful language

Success signal:

- at least 5 meaningful builder reactions
- at least 1 person asks for more detail or shares a real RAG problem

### Phase 2: Paid Validation

Goal: test whether someone pays for a manual decision review.

Tasks:

- offer the USD 99 RAG Architecture Review to qualified builders
- deliver one manual review
- record how hard it was, what data was missing, and what part felt valuable
- improve the intake and deliverable templates

Success signal:

- 1 paid review, preorder, or serious buyer request

### Phase 3: Repeatable Knowledge Product

Goal: turn repeated review patterns into reusable assets.

Tasks:

- refine decision cards
- add more RAG decisions
- assemble the RAG Decision Playbook outline
- publish weekly Architecture Diffs
- keep evidence reviews on a weekly cadence

Success signal:

- recurring audience pull
- repeated questions
- builder referrals
- demand for a fresher or deeper version

### Phase 4: Automation And Software

Goal: automate only the parts proven useful.

Possible builds:

- evidence ingestion assistant
- source diff monitor
- decision-change detector
- decision card generator
- CLI recommendation helper
- lightweight dashboard
- SaaS monitoring only after repeated demand

Do not start here. Start here only after manual decision work has pull.

## Realistic Work Remaining

Minimum useful validation:

- 5 to 10 hours to polish and share the first artifact
- 3 to 5 hours to message 5 to 10 builders
- 5 to 8 hours to process replies and revise positioning
- 4 to 8 hours to deliver one manual review if someone bites

Realistic first money:

- possible in 1 to 3 weeks if outreach happens and one builder has an active RAG problem
- more likely in 4 to 8 weeks if distribution is cold and trust must be built first

Realistic first meaningful product:

- 2 to 4 weeks for a credible RAG-001 public proof package
- 4 to 8 weeks for a small paid playbook or review workflow
- 2 to 4 months for a lightweight software tool worth maintaining

Realistic business risk:

- the audience may want implementation help instead of decision help
- the offer may feel too abstract
- the first public artifact may need sharper examples
- credibility will require consistent publishing and source discipline

## Money Path

Near-term:

- USD 99 custom RAG review
- target 1 to 3 early buyers
- learn whether the deliverable is worth improving

Mid-term:

- USD 19 to USD 49 RAG Decision Playbook
- USD 199 to USD 299 deeper architecture review
- weekly paid update or advisory format if people want freshness

Long-term:

- open-source decision library for trust and distribution
- paid fresher evidence, weekly diffs, deeper explanations, custom watchlists
- CLI or SaaS only after demand is proven

## Operating Rule

The next milestone is not to build more infrastructure.

The next milestone is to get one strong Baseplate artifact in front of builders and learn whether the decision layer is useful enough to pull money, replies, or real problems toward it.
