# Baseplate Week 1 Operating Plan

Date: 2026-05-16

Goal: validate whether RAG-001 is useful enough that builders engage, ask for more, or consider paying for a custom review.

## Week 1 Outcome

By the end of the week:

- Architecture Diff #001 is publish-ready.
- RAG-001 decision page is publish-ready.
- USD 99 custom RAG review offer is ready.
- 5 to 10 builder feedback conversations are started.
- At least 3 objections/pain points are recorded.

## Day 1: Finalize Assets

Tasks:

- Review `publishable-architecture-diff-001.md`.
- Review `decision-page-rag-001-public.md`.
- Review `custom-rag-review-offer.md`.
- Make sure every strong claim has a caveat and source.

Acceptance:

- The post can be shared without embarrassment.
- The offer can be explained in one sentence.

## Day 2: Publish One Useful Artifact

Publish only one thing:

- Architecture Diff #001, or
- the shorter RAG-001 decision page.

Do not publish both at once.

Goal:

- create one clean artifact to point people toward.

## Day 3: Ask For Feedback

Message 5 builders.

Do not sell immediately unless they show buying intent.

Ask:

> Would this have saved you time or prevented a bad RAG architecture choice?

Track:

- what they understood
- what confused them
- what decision they cared about
- whether they would pay for a review

## Day 4: Offer The Review

If someone has a real RAG project, offer the USD 99 review.

Position it as a test:

> I am testing a lightweight RAG architecture review. You answer a short intake form, and I return a practical architecture recommendation with caveats and what not to overbuild.

## Day 5: Improve From Objections

Update:

- public post
- review offer
- intake form
- decision page

Based on:

- repeated confusion
- repeated pains
- objections
- buyer language

## Day 6: Publish One Follow-Up

Possible follow-up posts:

- "When vector-only RAG is enough"
- "When GraphRAG is worth it"
- "The 30-question RAG eval set"
- "RAG access control is not prompt engineering"

## Day 7: Decide Next Move

Continue if:

- at least one builder asks for more detail
- one builder shares a specific RAG pain
- someone asks about the review
- the post gets thoughtful replies

Change positioning if:

- people say it is too obvious
- nobody understands the offer
- builders want implementation help, not decision help
- the audience is wrong

## Weekly Rule

One public decision/diff per week.

One evidence sweep per week.

Five builder conversations per week.

No SaaS or CLI until the decision layer gets real demand.
